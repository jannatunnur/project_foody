Burger

Made by : Jannatun Nur

Rating

Topic Sentence: The top bun of the paragraph is the topic sentence. It holds all the good stuff together and tells us what the paragraph is about. Think about it - if it's not half of a hamburger bun, then it's not a cheeseburger. A regular piece of bread turns it into a patty melt, just like a regular sentence would turn a paragraph into something else. Transition: Connected to the topic sentence is the transition. Often the transition is combined with the topic sentence, just as the ketchup, mustard, mayo, barbecue sauce and other delectable condiments are spread on the bun. The transition adds a unique "flavor" to the paragraph and makes it easier to move from one paragraph to the next. You might also include transition words and phrases in other parts of the paragraph, just like you might put cheese or barbecue sauce in other parts of the burger. Just another way to add some flavor. Details: The "meat" of the paragraph is the evidence - details, quotes, facts, examples, and so on. Just like a burger can use different spices in the meat, or even different meats entirely, so too can a paragraph use different kinds of details. These details are what make the paragraph meaningful. Just like a burger without meat is really just a salad and bread, a paragraph without details is...well, not good writing. And, of course, just like turning your burger into a double makes it better, adding more meaty details to your paragraph is sure to improve your writing, as well.
